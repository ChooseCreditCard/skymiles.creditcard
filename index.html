Delta SkyMiles: Miles, Status & Credit Cards

SkyMiles is Delta Air Lines’ loyalty program. This page explains how SkyMiles are earned, how elite status works, what you can redeem miles for, and how co-branded credit cards can become a major source of miles.

Best Cards for Earning SkyMiles

1. What the SkyMiles Program Is

SkyMiles is the frequent-flyer program of Delta Air Lines. Members earn miles when they fly with Delta or selected partners, and when they use Delta-linked credit cards and partners on the ground. Miles can then be redeemed for flights, upgrades and other travel-related rewards.

Like most airline programs, SkyMiles combines:

  • A mileage currency for redemptions on Delta and partner flights.
  • Elite status tiers that unlock extra benefits such as priority services and preferred seats.
  • Co-branded credit cards that tie everyday spending into the SkyMiles ecosystem.

2. How SkyMiles Are Earned

SkyMiles can be earned through several channels:

  • Delta-operated flights – miles typically based on the fare you pay and your elite status level.
  • Partner airline flights – flights on eligible partners credited to your SkyMiles account.
  • Co-branded credit cards – miles on every dollar spent, with higher earn rates in certain categories.
  • Hotels, car rentals and shopping – partner activity where you enter your SkyMiles number.
  • Promotions – occasional limited-time offers with bonus miles on specific routes or partners.

For many members, the majority of miles over time come from credit card spend rather than from flying itself.

3. How Credit Cards Boost SkyMiles Earnings

Delta-branded credit cards allow you to earn SkyMiles on everyday expenses. Depending on your market, typical card features include:

  • Miles on all purchases, often with elevated earn rates on Delta purchases, restaurants or groceries.
  • Welcome bonuses after you spend a required minimum within a set time period.
  • Flight perks such as priority boarding, free checked bags or companion certificates.
  • Status-related boosts, where spending can help you qualify for or keep higher tiers.

By routing your normal spending through the right card, you can build a SkyMiles balance even in years where you fly less.

4. Redemption Categories in SkyMiles

SkyMiles uses dynamic pricing rather than a simple fixed award chart, but the main redemption categories are:

  • Delta flights – one-way or return tickets paid partly or fully with miles.
  • Partner flights – redemptions on selected alliance and non-alliance partners.
  • Seat upgrades – upgrades from economy into higher cabins on some itineraries.
  • Vacation packages and extras – in some markets, miles can offset packages or add-ons.

The value you get per mile can vary widely. Many travelers aim to use SkyMiles for flights rather than small purchases or merchandise.

5. SkyMiles Elite Status Tiers

SkyMiles has several elite status levels that provide additional perks when you fly Delta and selected partners. Names, metrics and requirements may change over time, but the general structure is:

  • General Member – basic earning and redemption without extra perks.
  • Silver Medallion – entry-level elite with some priority and upgrade opportunities.
  • Gold Medallion – stronger upgrade priority and additional travel benefits.
  • Platinum Medallion – higher upgrade priority and more flexible benefits.
  • Diamond Medallion – top published tier with the most generous perks and recognition.

Some co-branded cards can help you on the way to these tiers by providing boosts, waiver options or extra earning on the metrics that qualify you for status.
